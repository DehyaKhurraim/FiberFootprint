Cotton

Despite its natural origin, cotton production is resource-intensive, consuming around 2,700 liters of water for just one t-shirt. The environmental costs include significant water usage, pesticide application, and soil depletion.

Polyester

Polyester, a synthetic fiber derived from petroleum, is associated with substantial energy consumption and microplastic pollution. These microplastics enter our oceans, impacting marine life and ecosystems.

Hemp

Hemp stands out as a more eco-friendly option, requiring minimal water and pesticides. It offers a durable, sustainable alternative with a lower environmental impact compared to other fibers.
